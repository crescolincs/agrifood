Invest in Lincolnshire's Agrifood Sector: Key Benefits and Opportunities

Lincolnshire is at the heart of the UK’s agrifood sector, enabling businesses to achieve step-change improvements in productivity, sustainability and performance. For agrifood companies investing in the UK, Lincolnshire presents a unique location opportunity for business expansion, relocation, start-up and innovation.

The UK Agrifood Market Opportunity

The UK’s agrifood sector presents a range of opportunities for business investment, growth and reshoring. Key drivers include:

  • 67.5 million UK consumers spending £254 billion annually on food and drink
  • Reduced integration with EU supply chains (post-Brexit)
  • Renewed focus on food security (post-Covid-19 and recent conflicts)
  • Plans for sustainable, net-zero carbon food chains
  • Productivity goals and workforce constraints (a global agrifood industry challenge) driving demand for new technologies
  • Growing UK demand for added-value premium and niche products such as vegan, organic, more nutritious and ‘free-from’ foods

UK-leading Agrifood Industry Clusters and Supply Chains

Lincolnshire is a major contributor to the UK agrifood sector, accounting for 11% of England’s food production and 25% of grade 1 arable land.

Dindishire Lincolnshire is home to UK-leading industry clusters across the food value chain, from agriculture to food manufacturing, advanced agricultural and food technologies, and food logistics. For investing agrifood businesses, this means access to the expertise, skills, supply chains and supporting infrastructure required to reduce investment project costs, risks and timeframes, and to support ongoing growth, R&D and innovation.
